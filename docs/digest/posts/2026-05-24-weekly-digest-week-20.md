## Papers This Week

### [V-JEPA 2.1: Unlocking Dense Features in Video Self-Supervised Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.14482)
*V-JEPA 2.1 enhances self-supervised learning for dense visual understanding in images and videos by applying a Dense Predictive Loss across all tokens and incorporating Deep Self-Supervision.*
The method introduces a Dense Predictive Loss applied to both masked and unmasked tokens, and incorporates Deep Self-Supervision by applying the loss hierarchically across multiple intermediate encoder layers. This resulted in an mAP of 7.71 on Ego4D for short-term object-interaction anticipation.

**Why it matters:** To improve dense feature quality in SSL, extending the predictive loss to cover all tokens (masked and unmasked) is crucial.

### [RefDecoder: Enhancing Visual Generation with Conditional Video Decoding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.15196)
*REFDECODER enhances visual generation by introducing a reference-conditioned video VAE decoder that injects high-fidelity reference image signals directly into the decoding process via reference attention, mitigating detail loss and inconsistency inherent in unconditional VAE decoders.*
REFDECODER uses reference attention to inject high-fidelity reference image signals into the decoder, and employs a latent token dropout strategy to force the decoder to recover spatial details from reference tokens. This approach yielded a +2.1 dB PSNR improvement on WebVid compared to VideoVAE+.

**Why it matters:** REFDECODER is a drop-in replacement for existing video VAE decoders, requiring no retraining of the diffusion model or freezing the encoder.

### [Can These Views Be One Scene? Evaluating Multiview 3D Consistency when 3D Foundation Models Hallucinate](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.18754)
*This paper introduces SysCON3D, a controlled benchmark, and a parametric family of neural metrics to diagnose and improve the robustness of ground-truth-free multiview 3D consistency evaluators against geometric hallucinations.*
The authors developed SysCON3D, a controlled benchmark, and a parametric neural metric family that decomposes evaluators into backbone, residual, and aggregation components. This led to MASt3R-W-IMQ being up to 3$\times$ more robust than MEt3R.

**Why it matters:** When evaluating 3D consistency without ground truth, be aware that learned metrics inherit the failure modes of their underlying reconstruction backbones.

### [Variance Reduction for Expectations with Diffusion Teachers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.21489)
*CARV is a compute-aware variance-accounting framework that introduces a hierarchical Monte Carlo estimator by amortizing expensive upstream computations over cheap diffusion-noise resamples, sharpened by timestep importance sampling and stratified-inverse-CDF construction.*
CARV implements a hierarchical Monte Carlo estimator by caching expensive upstream computations and resampling cheap diffusion noise. This framework achieved Effective Compute Multipliers (ECM) of 2-3$\times$ compared to uniform-IID sampling.

**Why it matters:** When upstream computation is expensive relative to denoising cost ($c_{render+encode} \gg c_{denoise}$), prioritize compute reuse by caching expensive states.

### [Tokenisation via Convex Relaxations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22821)
*ConvexTok formulates tokeniser construction as a linear program relaxation of an NP-hard integer program, allowing for the computation of near-optimal tokenisers and providing lower bounds on compression.*
The paper formulates tokeniser construction as a linear program (LP) derived from an equivalent integer program (IP), and proposes three rounding schemes to discretize the LP solution. The resulting tokenisers consistently outperformed other tokenisers on intrinsic metrics.

**Why it matters:** ConvexTok offers a mathematically rigorous way to approach tokeniser design by framing it as an optimization problem.

### [Explicit Stair Geometry Conditioning for Robust Humanoid Locomotion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.09944)
*This paper introduces an explicit stair geometry conditioning framework that uses interpretable parameters like step height, step depth, and current yaw angle to condition a PPO-based locomotion policy for robust humanoid stair climbing.*
The framework conditions a PPO-based policy directly on step height, step depth, and current yaw, enabling anticipatory gait modulation. The policy demonstrated an MAE($h_{step}$) of 0.6 cm across unseen stair geometries in simulation.

**Why it matters:** Explicitly parameterizing terrain geometry (step height, step depth) provides more structured and task-relevant information than implicit latent representations for locomotion.

### [Which Way Did It Move? Diagnosing and Overcoming Directional Motion Blindness in Video-LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22823)
*The paper introduces MODIRECT and DeltaDirect to diagnose and overcome directional motion blindness in Video-LLMs, which stems from a direction binding gap where motion signals are present but not reliably linked to verbal answers.*
The authors introduced DeltaDirect, a training-only projector-level objective that predicts normalized 2-D motion vectors from adjacent-frame feature deltas. This resulted in a motion direction accuracy of 85.4% on MODIRECT-SYNBENCH, up from 25.9% for the vanilla baseline.

**Why it matters:** Training objectives like DeltaDirect can strengthen low-level motion cues at the visual-language interface, improving performance.
