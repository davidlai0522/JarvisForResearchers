## Papers This Week

### [Representation Forcing for Bottleneck-Free Unified Multimodal Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31604)
*Representation Forcing (RF) is a technique that enables bottleneck-free unified multimodal models by forcing the decoder to autoregressively predict visual representations as intermediate tokens, guiding pixel diffusion within the same backbone.*
RF closes the quality gap of pixel-space image generation in UMMs by eliminating the need for a pretrained VAE. The pixel-space model utilizing RF achieved a GenEval Overall score of 0.84, matching the BAGEL baseline (0.82), and reached 0.88 with an LLM rewriter.

**Why it matters:** RF allows for the creation of end-to-end UMMs by internalizing the latent space, removing reliance on external VAEs.

### [Thinking in Blender: Staged Executable Inverse Graphics with Vision-Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02580)
*SEIG is a Staged Executable Inverse Graphics framework that reconstructs editable 3D scenes from a single image by progressively refining scene factors—geometry, materials, composition, and lighting—directly within executable Blender code space using a pretrained Vision-Language Model (VLM).*
SEIG decomposes inverse graphics into sequential stages mirroring professional 3D workflows, such as geometry, material, composition, and lighting. It incorporates a generator-verifier loop within each stage, requiring the verifier to return an explicit approval checklist for convergence.

**Why it matters:** Task decomposition is crucial for enabling general-purpose VLMs to perform complex inverse graphics tasks effectively.

### [Exploring Easy Boosts for Lidar Semantic Scene Completion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03992)
*This paper introduces a plug-and-play recipe that significantly boosts Lidar Semantic Scene Completion (SSC) performance by augmenting input point clouds with semantic pseudo-labels and visibility information derived from off-the-shelf techniques.*
Combining semantic pseudo-labels and visibility information resulted in an mIoU of 33.5, surpassing the SemCity-AE baseline (25.7 mIoU with $\text{+25.7 mIoU}$). The combination of these two priors was shown to be complementary, yielding further performance gains.

**Why it matters:** Simple input augmentations (semantic pseudo-labels and visibility information) can make older SSC models competitive with state-of-the-art systems.

### [TailLoR: Protecting Principal Components in Parameter-Efficient Continual Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06494)
*TailLoR is a geometrically aware low-rank adaptation method that uses a soft spectral penalty to protect the dominant singular components of pre-trained weights, thereby routing new task adaptations into the underutilized spectral 'tail' to mitigate interference in continual learning.*
TailLoR achieves an Overall Accuracy (OA) of 78.87 on the Standard CL Benchmark (Order 1), outperforming the ELLA baseline (78.09). The method preserves the privacy of task-specific parameters as it requires no access to adapters from prior tasks.

**Why it matters:** When applying PEFT in continual learning, structurally guiding updates based on the singular value decomposition (SVD) of the base weights is more effective than uniform or simple subspace constraints.

### [TempoVLA: Learning Speed-Controllable Vision-Language-Action Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06491)
*TempoVLA introduces a framework to give existing Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models explicit, bidirectional control over execution speed by combining Variable-Speed Trajectory Augmentation (VSTA) on the data side and speed conditioning on the model side.*
The combination of VSTA and speed conditioning consistently lifts the default 1x success rate in simulation and the real world, achieving a Replay success rate (SR) of 97.6% at 1x. The textual prefix method was identified as the simplest and most flexible deployment choice.

**Why it matters:** Speed control can be added to existing VLAs with minimal engineering effort, as demonstrated by the comparable performance of the three speed injection schemes.
