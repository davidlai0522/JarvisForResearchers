## Overview
This week's research highlights several distinct but related challenges in extending generative and predictive models to complex, real-world domains. A major theme is the adaptation of models to data-scarce or challenging environments, seen in "Wat3R," which uses semi-supervised learning to handle underwater degradation, and "RoboTTT," which extends context for robot policies. In the realm of generative modeling, papers tackle fundamental limitations: "The Seriality Gap in Video Diffusion Models" pinpoints a theoretical bottleneck in sequential prediction, while "PanoWorld" addresses memory and geometric consistency in panoramic generation using rotation-equivariant representations. Furthermore, the integration of large language models into generative pipelines is explored via "Read It Back," which repurposes MLLMs as training-free reward functions for Text-to-Image RL.

## Papers This Week

### [Wat3R: Underwater 3D Geometry Learning without Annotations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08772)
*Wat3R is a cross-domain semi-supervised learning framework that adapts feed-forward 3D reconstruction models from air to underwater scenes without requiring any annotated underwater data by leveraging unlabeled real underwater video footage and a cross-view consistency loss.*
The framework proposes Wat3R, a semi-supervised VGGT-based approach that generalizes to diverse underwater scenes. It achieves an RMSE of 0.290 compared to VGGT [40] on the Shuffle 10-view Evaluation.
**Why it matters:** Semi-supervised learning is a viable paradigm for adapting powerful 3D models to data-scarce domains like underwater vision.

### [PanoWorld: Real-World Panoramic Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09661)
*PanoWorld is a diffusion-based framework that achieves high-fidelity and controllable panoramic video generation by leveraging the rotation-equivariant property of omnidirectional representations through Dense Panoramic Ray-Conditioning (DPRC) and Geometry-aware Memory Augmentation (GMA).*
PanoWorld introduces DPRC for dense geometric conditioning and GMA for memory retrieval, enabling it to outperform Imagine360, Matrix-3D, and OmniRoam on the World360 benchmark.
**Why it matters:** Leveraging rotation-equivariance in omnidirectional representations is key to building robust long-range memory models.

### [Read It Back: Pretrained MLLMs Are Zero-Shot Reward Models for Text-to-Image Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11886)
*SpectraReward is a training-free reward function that leverages the image-text alignment ability of pretrained MLLMs by measuring the average image-conditioned prompt log-likelihood to guide text-to-image reinforcement learning.*
The method proposes SpectraReward, which turns any pretrained MLLM into a reward model by calculating the image-conditioned prompt-token likelihood. Using Self-SpectraReward, the policy achieved a +10.0 improvement on TIIF-Bench (BAGEL policy) over the baseline.
**Why it matters:** For T2I RL, leveraging the inherent image-text alignment of pretrained MLLMs via prompt-likelihood is a viable, training-free alternative to expensive RLHF.

### [The Seriality Gap in Video Diffusion Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13031)
*The paper identifies the 'seriality gap' in video diffusion models, demonstrating that their denoising process fails to provide scalable serial computation necessary for accurately predicting long chains of dependent events, such as those found in hard-sphere dynamics.*
The authors use hard-sphere dynamics to show that bidirectional video diffusion degrades as event chains lengthen, and this gap is not closed by additional denoising steps. The Rollout-5 Error $\Delta x(5)$ was 0.20 for the baseline and 0.24 for the tested configuration.
**Why it matters:** For tasks requiring sequential reasoning (like physical simulation), relying solely on increasing denoising steps in diffusion models is insufficient to overcome serial dependency bottlenecks.

### [From Pixels to States: Rethinking Interactive World Models as Game Engines](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14076)
*This paper examines interactive game world modeling by organizing existing approaches around the conventional action-state-observation loop, and introduces a scalable data engine for Black Myth: Wukong to support state-aware modeling.*
The work introduces a unified framework for interactive game world modeling grounded in the action-state-observation loop, analyzing four dimensions of interaction. They built a scalable data engine for Black Myth: Wukong, collecting over 90 hours of gameplay videos with frame-aligned player actions and ground-truth game states.
**Why it matters:** For building next-generation game engines, the interaction must be viewed through the lens of the action-state-observation loop, not just video generation.

### [RoboTTT: Context Scaling for Robot Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15275)
*RoboTTT is a robot model and training recipe that integrates Test-Time Training (TTT) into Vision-Language-Action (VLA) policies to scale visuomotor context up to 8K timesteps, enabling new capabilities like one-shot in-context imitation and on-the-fly policy improvement without increasing inference latency.*
RoboTTT scales visuomotor context to 8K timesteps, resulting in an 87% overall task performance improvement over the single-step context baseline. The Gear Bot task completion score reached 79% compared to GR00T N1.7 (single-step context).
**Why it matters:** TTT allows for dynamic, in-context adaptation by updating model parameters during inference, effectively compressing long histories into weight space.

### [MeanFlowNFT: Bringing Forward-Process RL to Average-Velocity Generators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15273)
*MeanFlowNFT introduces a forward-process Reinforcement Learning framework, MeanFlowNFT, that applies the DiffusionNFT objective to an induced instantaneous-velocity predictor derived from MeanFlow's average-velocity network, enabling efficient RL tuning for few-step generation.*
The framework applies the DiffusionNFT objective to an induced instantaneous-velocity predictor, theoretically guaranteeing that the optimum matches the DiffusionNFT improved-policy target. MeanFlowNFT achieved a VBench score of 84.33 on Wan2.1, surpassing the 50-step LongCat-Video RL baseline.
**Why it matters:** MeanFlowNFT allows leveraging powerful forward-process RL techniques (like DiffusionNFT) without requiring the computationally expensive reverse-process trajectories or likelihood estimation.

## Trends & Observations
* There is a clear trend toward decoupling complex generative tasks into specialized components, such as separating geometric conditioning (PanoWorld) or optimizing velocity space separately from sampling (MeanFlowNFT).
* Semi-supervised and self-rewarding mechanisms are emerging as practical solutions to data scarcity, whether in environmental adaptation ("Wat3R") or RL training ("Read It Back").
* For sequential prediction, the community is moving beyond simply increasing model size or denoising steps, as highlighted by the theoretical limitations identified in "The Seriality Gap in Video Diffusion Models."
* Robot policy scaling is being addressed by integrating dynamic, inference-time adaptation techniques like TTT ("RoboTTT") to handle long-context dependencies without prohibitive latency.
* The definition of a "world model" is broadening from pure video generation to encompass explicit state tracking and action-state loops, as detailed in "From Pixels to States."
