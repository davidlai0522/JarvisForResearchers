    This Week in Robotics — 2026, Week 23

    Generated by JarvisForResearchers Bot on 2026-06-14

    Overview

    This week's research shows a clear trend toward creating more robust, generalist systems by synthesizing specialized knowledge or by intelligently managing information flow. In robotics, HANDOFF addresses the complexity of whole-body control by distilling knowledge from specialized controllers into a compact 10-D command space. Similarly, in generative modeling, Mirage tackles the memory bottleneck in video world models by moving scene representation into the latent space, achieving significant speedups. Multimodal learning is being analyzed theoretically in "When to Align, When to Predict," which provides a framework for deciding between alignment and prediction strategies based on noise characteristics. Furthermore, practical application focuses on making large models more efficient and capable; Reroute offers a training-free way to recover pruned visual tokens in VLMs, while InterleaveThinker uses a multi-agent setup to retrofit image generators for complex sequential tasks.

    Papers This Week

    HANDOFF: Humanoid Agentic Task-Space Whole-Body Control via Distilled Complementary Teachers

    HANDOFF is a task-space whole-body humanoid controller distilled from three complementary specialists—motion tracking, locomotion, and fall-recovery—to accept a compact, explicit 10-D planner-facing command. The method uses multi-teacher KL distillation under a context-conditioned gating scheme to create a single controller. It demonstrated performance improvements over FALCON [17], achieving \(|\Delta v_x| = 0.06\) and \(|\Delta v_y| = 0.18\).

    Why it matters: A compact, explicit 10-D command space allows high-level planners (like VLMs) to interface directly with complex whole-body controllers without requiring dense kinematic streams.

    UniSHARP: Universal Sharp Monocular View Synthesis

    UniSHARP extends SHARP for universal monocular novel view synthesis by aligning images in a unified omnidirectional latent space, enabling rendering across perspective, wide-FoV, fisheye, and panoramic camera systems. The framework reformulates SHARP-style Gaussian prediction in ray-distance space and fuses 2D semantic encodings with 3D spatial features. This resulted in a PSNR of 21.556 and an SSIM of 0.674 compared to SHARP [12].

    Why it matters: For universal monocular synthesis, shifting representation from image-plane coordinates to a ray-distance space is crucial for handling heterogeneous camera models.

    Latent Spatial Memory for Video World Models

    Mirage introduces latent spatial memory, a persistent 3D cache storing scene information directly in the diffusion latent space, to enable geometrically consistent video world models while avoiding the computational and representational bottlenecks of traditional RGB point-cloud memories. Mirage utilizes depth-guided back-projection and occlusion-aware readout at latent resolution. This approach yielded an end-to-end video generation speedup of \(10.57\times\) faster than RGB point-cloud readout.

    Why it matters: For 3D-consistent video generation, moving memory storage from pixel space (RGB) to latent space significantly reduces computational overhead.

    When to Align, When to Predict: A Phase Diagram for Multimodal Learning

    This paper develops a unified linear framework and phase diagram to systematically determine when Cross-modal Alignment (CA) or Cross-modal Prediction (CP) succeeds or fails in multimodal representation learning under a spiked signal-plus-noise model with structured cross-modal nuisance correlation. The authors derived separation ratios \(\Delta_{CA}\) and \(\Delta_{CP}\) under the specified model. The analysis showed a subspace distance of \(\nu \approx 0.75\) for CA robustness versus \(\nu \approx 0.15\) for CP failure.

    Why it matters: CA is preferable when nuisance correlation is moderate, modality quality is uncertain, and modality-specific noise is large.

    Reroute, Don't Remove: Recoverable Visual Token Routing for Vision-Language Models

    Reroute introduces a training-free plug-in that replaces irreversible visual token removal in Vision-Language Models (VLMs) with recoverable routing, allowing deferred tokens to re-enter the candidate pool at subsequent decoder stages. The plug-in preserves the FLOPs and KV-cache budget of augmented pruning methods. On LLaVA-1.5-7B, it achieved an IoU of \(0.80\) compared to \(0.38\) for PDrop.

    Why it matters: If your VLM token reduction method relies on permanent deletion, consider Reroute to recover lost visual evidence.

    InterleaveThinker: Reinforcing Agentic Interleaved Generation

    InterleaveThinker is a novel multi-agent pipeline that endows any existing image generator with strong interleaved generation capabilities by employing a Planner agent to sequence instructions and a Critic agent to enforce step-wise correction. The framework uses a dual-reward strategy (accuracy and step-wise) to achieve trajectory-level alignment via GRPO. This resulted in a WISE score of \(0.73\) compared to a baseline of \(0.47\).

    Why it matters: Multi-agent frameworks can effectively retrofit existing, frozen image generators to handle complex sequential tasks like visual narratives.

    Learning to Reason by Analogy via Retrieval-Augmented Reinforcement Fine-Tuning

    RA-RFT is a post-training framework that enhances language model reasoning by using gold-relevance distillation to train a retriever that selects reasoning-analogous examples, which are then used to fine-tune the policy model via reinforcement learning. The retriever is trained to rank contexts by expected reasoning benefit rather than semantic overlap. This led to an AIME 2025 average@32 accuracy of \(7.1\) points when compared to \(2.8\) points using GRPO.

    Why it matters: For complex reasoning tasks, grounding retrieval in 'reasoning utility' (analogical fit) is more effective than relying on surface-level semantic similarity.

    Trends & Observations

    • There is a strong architectural trend toward distillation and synthesis, exemplified by HANDOFF creating a generalist controller from specialized components.
    • Memory management in generative models is shifting from high-dimensional pixel space to lower-dimensional latent space (Mirage), which offers substantial computational savings.
    • Theoretical work is providing prescriptive guidance for multimodal systems, suggesting that the choice between alignment and prediction depends on the underlying noise structure ("When to Align, When to Predict").
    • Efficiency improvements in large models are moving beyond simple pruning to methods that allow for information recovery (Reroute).
    • Complex agentic behavior is being achieved by decoupling planning from execution/evaluation using multi-agent architectures (InterleaveThinker).

    This Week in Robotics — 2026, Week 22

    Generated by JarvisForResearchers Bot on 2026-06-07

    Overview

    This week's papers touch on several distinct areas of modern AI, from unifying multimodal representations to enhancing robotic control and improving perception pipelines. A recurring theme is the efficacy of targeted, low-overhead modifications: Representation Forcing (RF) in "Representation Forcing for Bottleneck-Free Unified Multimodal Models" allows pixel-space models to match VAE-based counterparts without external components. Similarly, "Exploring Easy Boosts for Lidar Semantic Scene Completion" shows that simple input augmentations, like adding visibility priors, significantly elevate SSC performance. In the realm of continual learning, "TailLoR" introduces a spectral approach to PEFT, steering updates away from dominant components to preserve prior knowledge. Furthermore, "TempoVLA" addresses the need for temporal control in embodied AI, while "Thinking in Blender" demonstrates how structured decomposition enables VLMs to tackle complex, multi-stage inverse graphics tasks.

    Papers This Week

    Representation Forcing for Bottleneck-Free Unified Multimodal Models

    Representation Forcing (RF) is a technique that enables bottleneck-free unified multimodal models by forcing the decoder to autoregressively predict visual representations as intermediate tokens, guiding pixel diffusion within the same backbone. RF closes the quality gap of pixel-space image generation in UMMs by eliminating the need for a pretrained VAE. The pixel-space model utilizing RF achieved a GenEval Overall score of 0.84, matching the BAGEL baseline (0.82), and reached 0.88 with an LLM rewriter.

    Why it matters: RF allows for the creation of end-to-end UMMs by internalizing the latent space, removing reliance on external VAEs.

    Thinking in Blender: Staged Executable Inverse Graphics with Vision-Language Models

    SEIG is a Staged Executable Inverse Graphics framework that reconstructs editable 3D scenes from a single image by progressively refining scene factors—geometry, materials, composition, and lighting—directly within executable Blender code space using a pretrained Vision-Language Model (VLM). SEIG decomposes inverse graphics into sequential stages mirroring professional 3D workflows, such as geometry, material, composition, and lighting. It incorporates a generator-verifier loop within each stage, requiring the verifier to return an explicit approval checklist for convergence.

    Why it matters: Task decomposition is crucial for enabling general-purpose VLMs to perform complex inverse graphics tasks effectively.

    Exploring Easy Boosts for Lidar Semantic Scene Completion

    This paper introduces a plug-and-play recipe that significantly boosts Lidar Semantic Scene Completion (SSC) performance by augmenting input point clouds with semantic pseudo-labels and visibility information derived from off-the-shelf techniques. Combining semantic pseudo-labels and visibility information resulted in an mIoU of 33.5, surpassing the SemCity-AE baseline (25.7 mIoU with \(\text{+25.7 mIoU}\)). The combination of these two priors was shown to be complementary, yielding further performance gains.

    Why it matters: Simple input augmentations (semantic pseudo-labels and visibility information) can make older SSC models competitive with state-of-the-art systems.

    TailLoR: Protecting Principal Components in Parameter-Efficient Continual Learning

    TailLoR is a geometrically aware low-rank adaptation method that uses a soft spectral penalty to protect the dominant singular components of pre-trained weights, thereby routing new task adaptations into the underutilized spectral 'tail' to mitigate interference in continual learning. TailLoR achieves an Overall Accuracy (OA) of 78.87 on the Standard CL Benchmark (Order 1), outperforming the ELLA baseline (78.09). The method preserves the privacy of task-specific parameters as it requires no access to adapters from prior tasks.

    Why it matters: When applying PEFT in continual learning, structurally guiding updates based on the singular value decomposition (SVD) of the base weights is more effective than uniform or simple subspace constraints.

    TempoVLA: Learning Speed-Controllable Vision-Language-Action Policies

    TempoVLA introduces a framework to give existing Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models explicit, bidirectional control over execution speed by combining Variable-Speed Trajectory Augmentation (VSTA) on the data side and speed conditioning on the model side. The combination of VSTA and speed conditioning consistently lifts the default 1x success rate in simulation and the real world, achieving a Replay success rate (SR) of 97.6% at 1x. The textual prefix method was identified as the simplest and most flexible deployment choice.

    Why it matters: Speed control can be added to existing VLAs with minimal engineering effort, as demonstrated by the comparable performance of the three speed injection schemes.

    Trends & Observations

    • There is a clear trend toward augmenting existing systems rather than building entirely new ones, seen in the "Easy Boosts" for Lidar and the speed injection in "TempoVLA."
    • Spectral analysis (SVD) is emerging as a powerful tool for structural modification in PEFT, as demonstrated by "TailLoR" for continual learning.
    • Unified Multimodal Models (UMMs) are moving away from rigid latent space assumptions, with "Representation Forcing" enabling end-to-end pixel-space integration.
    • Complex, real-world tasks (like 3D reconstruction) are being addressed by imposing structured, staged workflows onto general-purpose VLMs, as shown in "Thinking in Blender."
    • The integration of temporal control (speed) is becoming a recognized, low-cost extension for embodied AI policies.

    This Week in Robotics — 2026, Week 21

    Generated by JarvisForResearchers Bot on 2026-05-31

    Overview

    This week's papers span diverse domains, from economic modeling to embodied AI and scientific software engineering, revealing consistent themes around structural rigor and controlled optimization. In economics, "Integrable Elasticity via Neural Demand Potentials" moves beyond standard regression by enforcing analytical tractability through a demand-first formulation. For robotics, "Latent Action Diffusion for Cross-Embodiment Manipulation" addresses the challenge of skill transfer across heterogeneous hardware by aligning action spaces in a latent manifold. A parallel theme of systematic refinement appears in "SkillOpt: Executive Strategy for Self-Evolving Agent Skills," which optimizes procedural knowledge externally without altering core model weights. Furthermore, the reliability of AI systems is scrutinized in two ways: "TriSplat" achieves simulation readiness by natively representing geometry with oriented triangles, while "Physics Is All You Need?" emphasizes that rigorous human supervision dictates the trustworthiness of scientific AI outputs.

    Papers This Week

    Integrable Elasticity via Neural Demand Potentials

    The Integrable Context-Dependent Demand Network (ICDN) is a demand-first neural model that represents log-demand as a smooth, context-conditioned function of log-prices, allowing elasticities to be derived exactly as derivatives of the learned demand surface. ICDN decomposes price responses into linear own-price effects, directed cross-price interactions, and spline-based nonlinear refinements. It computes own- and cross-price elasticities by exploiting analytic derivatives of product-specific spline bases, avoiding dense Jacobian or Hessian evaluations. Why it matters: ICDN provides a framework to derive elasticities exactly from a single, smooth demand surface, enhancing analytical tractability.

    SkillOpt: Executive Strategy for Self-Evolving Agent Skills

    SkillOpt introduces a systematic, controllable text-space optimizer that treats agent skills as an external state, allowing for deep-learning-style optimization of procedural knowledge without modifying the frozen target model weights. The framework uses rollout batches, reflection minibatches, and textual learning rates to optimize skills. Across its empirical study, SkillOpt achieved an average gain of +23.5 points over no skill when using GPT-5.5 in a direct chat setting. Why it matters: SkillOpt provides a framework to systematically optimize agent procedures (skills) using deep-learning optimization principles without requiring access to model weights.

    TriSplat: Simulation-Ready Feed-Forward 3D Scene Reconstruction

    TriSplat is a feed-forward 3D scene reconstruction network that uses oriented triangle primitives to directly export simulation-ready mesh scenes from sparse, unposed images in a single forward pass. The network jointly predicts geometry, appearance, and camera poses using oriented triangle primitives. This triangle-native representation eliminates post-hoc mesh extraction, allowing direct consumption by physics engines. Why it matters: For simulation-ready 3D reconstruction, using explicit surface primitives (like triangles) natively in a feed-forward pipeline avoids costly post-processing steps.

    Latent Action Diffusion for Cross-Embodiment Manipulation

    This paper introduces Latent Action Diffusion to unify diverse end-effector action spaces into a semantically aligned latent space, enabling cross-embodiment policy learning for multi-robot control and skill transfer. The method uses contrastive learning to unify diverse action spaces into a single latent space. Factorizing policies into a latent, embodiment-agnostic policy and embodiment-specific action decoders resulted in up to a 25.3% improvement in manipulation success rate over single-embodiment policies. Why it matters: Use contrastive learning to bridge heterogeneous action spaces when transferring skills between robots with different end-effectors.

    Algorithmic Monocultures in Hiring

    This study investigates algorithmic monoculture in hiring by analyzing a novel dataset from the vendor pymetrics, finding clear racial disparities and systemic rejection patterns when examining hiring decisions on a per-position basis. The research provided evidence of systemic rejections at scale due to deployed hiring algorithms. Specifically, the adverse impact against Black applicants per position was measured at 10.62%. Why it matters: Analyzing hiring algorithms on a per-position basis is critical when assessing vendor-mediated systems, as aggregate metrics can mask localized discrimination.

    Physics Is All You Need? A Case Study in Physicist-Supervised AI Development of Scientific Software

    This case study details how a physicist supervised an AI coding agent (Claude Code, Sonnet, and Opus) over 12 work days and 57 sessions to build a differentiable one-loop perturbation theory module (CLAX-PT \(\S\)), demonstrating that the supervision protocol, not model capability, was key to achieving trustworthy scientific software. The study identified that the supervision protocol was the primary factor in the agent's output trustworthiness. The agent achieved \(\pm 1\%\) accuracy against the established CLASS-PT benchmark. Why it matters: For scientific AI development, the supervision protocol is as critical as the model's raw capability.

    Trends & Observations

    • There is a trend toward enforcing analytical constraints within neural models, as seen in "Integrable Elasticity via Neural Demand Potentials" and the explicit geometric constraints in "TriSplat."
    • Control and skill transfer across heterogeneous systems are being addressed by abstracting away physical differences, exemplified by the latent space unification in "Latent Action Diffusion."
    • The role of external, structured optimization is gaining traction; "SkillOpt" demonstrates optimizing procedural knowledge outside the core model weights, while "Physics Is All You Need?" shows external human protocols guiding AI output.
    • Methodological rigor in evaluation is a recurring theme, whether it is using counterfactuals in "Algorithmic Monocultures in Hiring" or testing at diverse parameter points in "Physics Is All You Need?".
    • The shift from post-hoc processing to native representation is evident, with "TriSplat" embedding geometry directly into its forward pass structure.

    This Week in Robotics — 2026, Week 20

    Generated by JarvisForResearchers Bot on 2026-05-24

    Overview

    This week's research shows a strong focus on refining the fidelity and interpretability of complex generative and predictive models across vision and robotics. In self-supervised learning, V-JEPA 2.1 demonstrates that applying Dense Predictive Loss across all tokens, coupled with Deep Self-Supervision, yields measurable gains in anticipation tasks. On the generation side, REFDECODER addresses detail loss in video VAEs by explicitly conditioning the decoder on high-fidelity reference signals, achieving PSNR improvements over existing methods. Furthermore, the field is grappling with the limitations of learned metrics; SysCON3D provides a necessary framework to test 3D consistency evaluators against geometric hallucinations. Finally, several papers tackle specific bottlenecks: CARV optimizes variance reduction in diffusion sampling, ConvexTok offers a rigorous optimization approach to tokenization, and MODIRECT targets the directional motion blindness in Video-LLMs.

    Papers This Week

    V-JEPA 2.1: Unlocking Dense Features in Video Self-Supervised Learning

    V-JEPA 2.1 enhances self-supervised learning for dense visual understanding in images and videos by applying a Dense Predictive Loss across all tokens and incorporating Deep Self-Supervision. The method introduces a Dense Predictive Loss applied to both masked and unmasked tokens, and incorporates Deep Self-Supervision by applying the loss hierarchically across multiple intermediate encoder layers. This resulted in an mAP of 7.71 on Ego4D for short-term object-interaction anticipation.

    Why it matters: To improve dense feature quality in SSL, extending the predictive loss to cover all tokens (masked and unmasked) is crucial.

    RefDecoder: Enhancing Visual Generation with Conditional Video Decoding

    REFDECODER enhances visual generation by introducing a reference-conditioned video VAE decoder that injects high-fidelity reference image signals directly into the decoding process via reference attention, mitigating detail loss and inconsistency inherent in unconditional VAE decoders. REFDECODER uses reference attention to inject high-fidelity reference image signals into the decoder, and employs a latent token dropout strategy to force the decoder to recover spatial details from reference tokens. This approach yielded a +2.1 dB PSNR improvement on WebVid compared to VideoVAE+.

    Why it matters: REFDECODER is a drop-in replacement for existing video VAE decoders, requiring no retraining of the diffusion model or freezing the encoder.

    Can These Views Be One Scene? Evaluating Multiview 3D Consistency when 3D Foundation Models Hallucinate

    This paper introduces SysCON3D, a controlled benchmark, and a parametric family of neural metrics to diagnose and improve the robustness of ground-truth-free multiview 3D consistency evaluators against geometric hallucinations. The authors developed SysCON3D, a controlled benchmark, and a parametric neural metric family that decomposes evaluators into backbone, residual, and aggregation components. This led to MASt3R-W-IMQ being up to 3\(\times\) more robust than MEt3R.

    Why it matters: When evaluating 3D consistency without ground truth, be aware that learned metrics inherit the failure modes of their underlying reconstruction backbones.

    Variance Reduction for Expectations with Diffusion Teachers

    CARV is a compute-aware variance-accounting framework that introduces a hierarchical Monte Carlo estimator by amortizing expensive upstream computations over cheap diffusion-noise resamples, sharpened by timestep importance sampling and stratified-inverse-CDF construction. CARV implements a hierarchical Monte Carlo estimator by caching expensive upstream computations and resampling cheap diffusion noise. This framework achieved Effective Compute Multipliers (ECM) of 2-3\(\times\) compared to uniform-IID sampling.

    Why it matters: When upstream computation is expensive relative to denoising cost (\(c_{render+encode} \gg c_{denoise}\)), prioritize compute reuse by caching expensive states.

    Tokenisation via Convex Relaxations

    ConvexTok formulates tokeniser construction as a linear program relaxation of an NP-hard integer program, allowing for the computation of near-optimal tokenisers and providing lower bounds on compression. The paper formulates tokeniser construction as a linear program (LP) derived from an equivalent integer program (IP), and proposes three rounding schemes to discretize the LP solution. The resulting tokenisers consistently outperformed other tokenisers on intrinsic metrics.

    Why it matters: ConvexTok offers a mathematically rigorous way to approach tokeniser design by framing it as an optimization problem.

    Explicit Stair Geometry Conditioning for Robust Humanoid Locomotion

    This paper introduces an explicit stair geometry conditioning framework that uses interpretable parameters like step height, step depth, and current yaw angle to condition a PPO-based locomotion policy for robust humanoid stair climbing. The framework conditions a PPO-based policy directly on step height, step depth, and current yaw, enabling anticipatory gait modulation. The policy demonstrated an MAE(\(h_{step}\)) of 0.6 cm across unseen stair geometries in simulation.

    Why it matters: Explicitly parameterizing terrain geometry (step height, step depth) provides more structured and task-relevant information than implicit latent representations for locomotion.

    Which Way Did It Move? Diagnosing and Overcoming Directional Motion Blindness in Video-LLMs

    The paper introduces MODIRECT and DeltaDirect to diagnose and overcome directional motion blindness in Video-LLMs, which stems from a direction binding gap where motion signals are present but not reliably linked to verbal answers. The authors introduced DeltaDirect, a training-only projector-level objective that predicts normalized 2-D motion vectors from adjacent-frame feature deltas. This resulted in a motion direction accuracy of 85.4% on MODIRECT-SYNBENCH, up from 25.9% for the vanilla baseline.

    Why it matters: Training objectives like DeltaDirect can strengthen low-level motion cues at the visual-language interface, improving performance.

    Trends & Observations

    • There is a clear trend toward making learned metrics more robust by moving beyond simple aggregation, as seen in the development of parametric neural metrics in SysCON3D.
    • Several papers address the efficiency of deep learning pipelines, whether through compute amortization (CARV) or by providing mathematically grounded design choices (ConvexTok).
    • The integration of explicit, interpretable geometric parameters (e.g., step height, step depth) is proving more effective for control tasks than relying solely on implicit latent representations.
    • Video understanding research is focusing on fine-grained signal binding, specifically addressing directional motion blindness in Video-LLMs (MODIRECT).
    • Self-supervised learning advancements (V-JEPA 2.1) are pushing the boundary of feature density by modifying the loss function application across all tokens.

    This Week in Robotics — 2026, Week 17

    Generated by JarvisForResearchers Bot on 2026-05-01

    Overview

    This week's submissions highlight advancements across perception, world modeling, and real-time interaction, demonstrating a trend toward unified, feed-forward systems. OmniRobotHome addresses the complexity of real-time, multiadic interaction in unstructured home settings, showing that wide-area sensing and behavioral modeling improve safety metrics. In the domain of autonomous driving, HERMES++ tackles the integration challenge by unifying 3D scene understanding and future geometry prediction within a BEV framework, enhanced by LLM queries. Complementing this, GenWildSplat pushes the boundaries of 3D reconstruction by offering a feed-forward solution for sparse, unconstrained images, bypassing the computational overhead of per-scene optimization. A cross-cutting theme is the move away from iterative optimization toward integrated, single-pass inference pipelines for robustness and speed.

    Papers This Week

    OmniRobotHome: A Multi-Camera Platform for Real-Time Multiadic Human-Robot Interaction

    OmniRobotHome is a room-scale platform integrating 48 hardware-synchronized RGB cameras and two Franka arms to enable real-time, occlusion-robust multiadic human-robot interaction in a natural home environment. The system employs an end-to-end real-time sensing pipeline, achieving an Avg. Cycle time of 63.12s with a Dynamic Policy compared to a Non-aware baseline. Furthermore, the Dynamic Policy achieved 28.5 Human Hits, indicating improved interaction robustness.

    Why it matters: For complex, multi-agent tasks in unstructured environments, real-time, room-scale 3D perception is the critical enabler.

    HERMES++: Toward a Unified Driving World Model for 3D Scene Understanding and Generation

    HERMES++ is a unified driving world model that integrates 3D scene understanding and future geometry prediction within a single framework by leveraging a Bird’s-Eye View (BEV) representation and LLM-enhanced world queries. The framework utilizes a Joint Geometric Optimization strategy to enforce structural integrity, resulting in an 8.2% reduction in 3s point cloud generation error compared to DriveX [18]. It also shows a 9.2% outperformance on the CIDEr metric for understanding tasks versus Omni-Q [15].

    Why it matters: Unified world models are necessary to bridge the gap between semantic interpretation (LLMs) and physical simulation (geometry prediction).

    Generalizable Sparse-View 3D Reconstruction from Unconstrained Images

    GenWildSplat is a feed-forward framework that achieves generalizable 3D scene reconstruction from sparse, unposed internet images by integrating appearance adaptation and occlusion modeling without requiring per-scene optimization. The framework achieves state-of-the-art feed-forward rendering quality while maintaining an Inference Time of 3 seconds. This is accomplished by using an appearance adapter modulated by a light code estimated by a light encoder.

    Why it matters: For real-time 3D reconstruction from unconstrained images, feed-forward models that avoid per-scene optimization are superior to optimization-based methods.

    Trends & Observations

    • There is a clear architectural trend toward unified models (HERMES++) that merge disparate capabilities, such as semantic understanding and geometric prediction, into a single framework.
    • Feed-forward inference is emerging as a preferred paradigm for perception tasks (GenWildSplat), offering significant speed advantages over traditional optimization-based methods.
    • The integration of large language models (LLMs) is being leveraged not just for high-level reasoning, but to directly guide physical simulation and scene evolution (HERMES++).
    • Robustness in complex environments is being addressed through comprehensive sensing (OmniRobotHome) and explicit modeling of physical constraints (HERMES++).
    • Decoupling appearance from geometry (GenWildSplat) allows for controllable rendering under novel environmental conditions, such as different lighting.
